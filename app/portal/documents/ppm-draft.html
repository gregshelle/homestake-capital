2.1 Risks Related to the Company's Business and Industry

2.1.1 Limited Operating History

The Company has a limited operating history and may not be able to successfully implement its business plan. There can be no assurance that the Company will achieve profitability or that it will be able to execute its acquisition strategy.

2.1.2 Acquisition Risks

The Company's growth strategy depends on identifying, acquiring, and integrating complementary businesses. The Company may be unable to identify suitable acquisition targets, negotiate acceptable terms, or successfully integrate acquired businesses. Acquisitions involve numerous risks, including:

  • Difficulties in integrating operations, technologies, and personnel
  • Diversion of management's attention from other business concerns
  • Assumption of unknown liabilities
  • Failure to achieve anticipated synergies or cost savings
  • Loss of key employees or customers of acquired businesses

2.1.3 Industry-Specific Risks

The home services industry is subject to various risks, including:

  • Seasonal fluctuations in demand
  • Economic downturns affecting discretionary spending on home services
  • Competition from established and new market entrants
  • Changes in consumer preferences and technology
  • Supply chain disruptions affecting equipment and materials
  • Labor shortages and rising wage costs
  • Regulatory changes affecting licensing and operations

2.1.4 Dependence on Key Personnel

The Company's success depends substantially on the continued service and performance of its executive officers and key employees. The loss of any key personnel could materially adversely affect the Company's business, financial condition, and results of operations.

10. INVESTOR SUITABILITY STANDARDS

[DRAFT - ATTORNEY REVIEW REQUIRED: Suitability standards must be reviewed by securities counsel to ensure compliance with applicable law]

10.1 Accredited Investor Requirement

This offering is available only to persons who qualify as "accredited investors" under Rule 501 of Regulation D. The Company will verify accredited investor status before accepting any subscription.

10.2 Accredited Investor Categories

Generally, an accredited investor is a person who meets one of the following criteria:

  • Individual Income Test: Natural person with income exceeding $200,000 in each of the two most recent years (or $300,000 together with spouse) and reasonably expects the same income level in the current year
  • Individual Net Worth Test: Natural person with net worth exceeding $1,000,000 (individually or with spouse), excluding primary residence
  • Entity Tests: Certain entities with assets exceeding $5,000,000, or all equity owners are accredited investors

10.3 Verification Process

The Company will verify accredited investor status through:

  • Third-party verification services
  • Review of tax returns, bank statements, or brokerage statements
  • Written confirmation from registered broker-dealers, registered investment advisers, or licensed attorneys
