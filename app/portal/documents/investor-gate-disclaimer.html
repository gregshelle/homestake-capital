What is Regulation D, Rule 506(c)?

Regulation D provides exemptions from the registration requirements of the Securities Act, allowing companies to offer and sell securities without registering with the SEC.

Rule 506(c) permits issuers to broadly solicit and generally advertise an offering, provided that:

  • All purchasers in the offering are accredited investors
  • The issuer takes reasonable steps to verify purchasers' accredited investor status
  • All other terms and conditions of Regulation D are satisfied

Accredited Investor Criteria

You qualify as an accredited investor if you meet at least one of the following criteria:

💰 Income Test (Individual)

Individual income exceeding $200,000 in each of the two most recent years (or $300,000 jointly with spouse) and reasonably expects the same income level in the current year.

🏠 Net Worth Test

Net worth exceeding $1,000,000 (individually or jointly with spouse), excluding the value of your primary residence.

🏢 Entity Test (Assets)

An entity with total assets in excess of $5,000,000 that was not formed for the specific purpose of acquiring the securities offered.

👥 Entity Test (Owners)

An entity in which all of the equity owners are accredited investors.
